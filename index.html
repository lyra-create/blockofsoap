The Process

Six weeks.
No shortcuts.

Most commercial soap is made in hours. Ours takes six weeks to cold-cure — a slow saponification that preserves every benefit of the oils, the camel milk, the clay. The result is a bar that's gentle enough for your children and luxurious enough for you.

Day 1 — The Pour

Fresh camel milk meets olive oil, coconut oil, and shea butter. Pink kaolin clay is folded in. A thin line of activated charcoal marks the divide before the second pour.

Day 2 — The Cut

Each batch is hand-cut into individual blocks and stamped with its serial number. Your bar gets its identity.

Weeks 1–6 — The Cure

Bars rest on wooden racks, turned weekly. Moisture evaporates. The bar hardens. pH drops. What emerges is soap the way it was meant to be.

Week 7 — The Wrap

Each bar is wrapped by hand in fabric sourced from local UAE artisans. No two wraps are alike.

Common Questions

Before you ask.

I've never used a bar soap before. Will it feel weird?

If you've only used liquid soap or body wash, a bar can feel different at first — in a good way. Our cold-process soap lathers rich and creamy (that's the camel milk and coconut oil), rinses clean without residue, and leaves your skin feeling moisturised, not stripped. Most people who switch never go back.

Is this actually better than regular soap?

Commercial "soap" is usually a detergent bar — synthetic surfactants that strip your skin's natural oils, plus artificial fragrance, preservatives, and hardeners. Our soap is actual soap: oils + lye → saponification. The glycerin (a natural by-product that most commercial manufacturers extract and sell separately) stays in the bar. That's what makes cold-process soap genuinely moisturising.

What about the camel milk? What does it actually do?

Camel milk is uniquely suited for skincare. It contains natural alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs) that gently exfoliate, vitamin C for brightening, lactoferrin with anti-inflammatory properties, and immunoglobulins that protect skin. It's been used in desert skincare for centuries — we just put it in a bar. Clinical studies confirm camel milk's moisturising and antimicrobial properties surpass cow and goat milk in cosmetic applications.

Is it safe for sensitive skin? Eczema?

Yes — this is one of the gentlest soaps you can use. Pink kaolin clay is the mildest of all clays, camel milk naturally soothes irritation, and shea butter locks in moisture. There are no synthetic fragrances, no parabens, no SLS. We leave it unscented specifically so it's safe for sensitive and reactive skin, including eczema-prone skin. That said, everyone's skin is different — patch test if you're unsure.

Why does it take 6 weeks?

Cold-process saponification is a chemical reaction that takes time. During the 6-week cure, moisture slowly evaporates (making the bar harder and longer-lasting), excess alkalinity drops to skin-safe pH levels, and the crystal structure of the soap matures. Rushing this process means a softer bar that dissolves quickly and may still be too alkaline. Patience literally makes better soap.

How long does one bar last?

With proper care — using a draining soap dish and keeping it dry between uses — a 200g bar typically lasts 4-6 weeks of daily use. That's why we include a free diatomite stone tray: it absorbs moisture and keeps your bar dry. We also have a soap care guide with tips to maximise longevity.

Why 97 bars? Why not more?

We make 100 bars per batch. Three stay with us — for our family, for quality testing, and to prove we use what we sell. 97 go to customers. This isn't artificial scarcity — it's the natural limit of our cold-process setup and our commitment to handling every bar personally. We'd rather make a small amount really well than a large amount that's just okay.

What's the charcoal line on the side?

It's our signature. During the pour, we lay a pencil-thin line of activated charcoal between the cream (camel milk) layer and the pink (kaolin clay) layer. It's visible on the side edge of every bar — a natural divider that also gently detoxifies. It's the fingerprint of a block. BODY. bar.

Can I use it on my face?

Yes. The ingredients are gentle enough for facial use. Camel milk's AHAs provide mild exfoliation, pink clay draws out impurities without drying, and the natural glycerin moisturises. Some people with very dry or reactive facial skin may prefer to use it every other day rather than daily. Listen to your skin.
